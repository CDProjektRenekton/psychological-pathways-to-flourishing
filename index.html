Introduction & Informed Consent
Greetings! I am Jolina U. Villalobos, RPm, currently enrolled in the Master of Arts in Psychology program at Mapúa University-Makati Campus. As part of the requirements for my degree, I am conducting a study entitled:

"Psychological Pathways Linking Physical Activity and Flourishing: A Moderated Mediation Model through Burnout and Social Support among Filipino Workers."

This study seeks to examine how physical activity relates to flourishing, with particular attention to the mediating role of burnout and the moderating role of social support in the workplace. The findings aim to benefit Filipino workers, organizations, and mental health practitioners by providing psychological insights that can inform resilience-building behaviors, workplace wellness programs, and evidence-based interventions to promote well-being and optimal functioning.

Purpose of the Study

This research aims to explore the psychological pathways linking physical activity and flourishing, focusing on burnout and social support among Filipino workers.

Procedures

Risks and Benefits

While you may not directly benefit, your responses will contribute to advancing psychological research and may inform workplace practices that promote well-being. This data collection has been approved by the Mapúa University Institutional Ethics Committee.
